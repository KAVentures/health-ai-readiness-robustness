# Internal Ethics Self-Assessment: Applicability of the Swedish Ethical Review Act

**Study:** "Evaluating medical AI under missing information: same-provider judges and human raters change apparent safety"

**Research principal:** Kinvectum AB, Sweden

**Author:** Koyar Afrasyab, MD

**Date of assessment:** 18 July 2026

## Assessment

Kinvectum AB, as research principal, assessed the project under the Swedish Ethical Review Act (2003:460) as in force on the date above. The project was assessed as falling outside the mandatory ethical-review provisions of sections 3 and 4. No application for ethical approval was therefore submitted to the Swedish Ethical Review Authority.

This is an internal assessment by the research principal and is not a decision or approval issued by the Swedish Ethical Review Authority.

## Basis for the assessment

### 1. Public benchmark data and model-generated outputs

The study used publicly released benchmark data from HealthBench, MedQA and MedMCQA, together with text generated by commercial large language models. The study team did not access source medical records, contact patients, or attempt to identify or re-identify any person represented in the benchmark material.

The benchmark data were analysed only in the form publicly distributed by their respective publishers. No direct patient identifiers were intentionally collected, processed or released by the study.

### 2. No sensitive personal data used as research variables

The project did not intentionally process sensitive personal data or personal data concerning criminal offences relating to an identifiable research participant. The clinical content of the benchmark questions was not linked by the study team to identified or reasonably identifiable individuals.

Limited ordinary personal data relating to the clinician contributors, such as contact information, professional role and consent documentation, were processed for study administration. These data were not research outcomes and are not publicly released.

### 3. Clinician annotation

Two independent clinicians and the author provided blinded professional annotations for a 50-item evaluation subset. The study analysed the resulting labels and inter-rater agreement, but did not investigate the clinicians' health, personal characteristics or behaviour outside the annotation task.

The annotation procedure involved no physical intervention, no method intended to influence the contributors physically or psychologically, and no evident risk of physical or psychological harm. No patient data beyond the public benchmark material were shown to the clinicians.

The independent clinicians gave explicit consent to:

1. use of their annotations for this research;
2. analysis of agreement between raters; and
3. public release of their annotations under coded rater identifiers.

The public replication package identifies the clinicians only by coded labels. The identity mapping and consent records are retained separately by the research principal and are not publicly released.

### 4. No biological material or clinical intervention

The study involved no biological material, physical procedures, patient contact, clinical intervention, prospective care decisions or assignment of treatment.

## Conclusion

The research principal assessed that the project did not involve:

- research processing of sensitive personal data or criminal-offence data concerning identifiable individuals;
- physical or psychological intervention involving a research person;
- an evident risk of physical or psychological harm; or
- identifiable human biological material.

The project was therefore assessed as not requiring mandatory ethical review under sections 3 and 4 of the Swedish Ethical Review Act (2003:460).

This assessment does not constitute ethical approval. If an editor or other authority requires external confirmation, the research principal will contact the Swedish Ethical Review Authority regarding the availability of an advisory opinion or other appropriate documentation. No retrospective ethical approval will be claimed.

## Research scope

The study evaluates model behaviour, prompt sensitivity, LLM-judge reliability and calibration against clinician annotations. It does not establish clinical deployment readiness, autonomous-care safety, clinical effectiveness or patient-outcome benefit.

## Retention

This assessment, the clinician consent records and the identity-code mapping are retained in the internal study records. The self-assessment is included in the replication package, while identifiable contributor information remains confidential.
